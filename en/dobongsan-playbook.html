Dobongsan Hiking Playbook

P
Depart from Songchu 2nd Parking Lot
Altitude 160m · 5-minute walk from bus stop

Gupabal Station (Subway Line 3) → Bus No. 34 → Get off at Songchu Valley (approx. 30 min). 5-minute walk to Obong Information Center. Check restrooms and maps at the information center.

🅿 Recommended to arrive before 8 AM on weekends. When driving, use Songchu Parking Lot 1 or 2.
1
Obong Visitor Center → Uldae Wetland
1.2km · 25min · ★☆☆☆☆

Gentle valley path. Passes through Uldae Wetland wooden deck. Well-maintained signposts ensure safety.

Stretching and warm-up exercises recommended at Uldae Wetland. Very cool in summer.
2
Uldae Wetland → Yeoseongbong Peak (504m)
1.5km · 50min · ★★★☆☆

The incline gradually increases. There is a rope section before the rock ridge. The panoramic view of Obong's five peaks from the summit is superb.

Yeoseongbong Peak = a scenic spot for panoramic views of Obong Peak. Recommended to depart after sufficient rest.
Caution: Slippery on rocky ridge sections. Children must hold hands.
3
Yeoseongbong Peak → Obong Peak (660m)
0.8km · 40min · ★★★★☆

Traverse 5 Obong peaks along the ridge. Rope climbing sections. Panoramic views of Seoul and Bukhansan National Park from each peak.

🪨 Obong: Hold the safety rope and move slowly. The 3rd peak is the highest.
4
Obong Peak → Songchupokpo Falls → Starting Point
3.5km · 70min · ★★☆☆☆

Steep descent, then via Songchu Falls. Return to starting point along the valley.

💧 After descent, highly recommend Pajeon and Makgeolli at Songchu Valley restaurant area!

Total round trip approx. 7km · 3~4h · Dobongsan Station loop

P
Depart from Dobongsan Station
Altitude 75m · Subway Line 1 & 7, Exit 1

Dobongsan Station (Subway Line 1 & 7), Exit 1 → Pass through the Outdoor Specialty Street → 15-minute walk to Dobong Information Center.

🛒 Final purchase of water and snacks at the convenience store on the specialized street before departure
1
Visitor Center → Dobongseowon Site
0.8km · 20min · ★☆☆☆☆

Gentle along the valley. The site of Dobongseowon Confucian Academy is a trace of the academy dedicated to Jo Gwang-jo, a Joseon Neo-Confucian scholar.

📜 Rock carvings remain, making it a historical site. Recommended to take a moment to appreciate.
2
Dobongseowon → Cheonchuksa Temple → Madangbawi Rock
1.5km · 50min · ★★★☆☆

Via Cheonchuksa Temple. Granite exposure section begins. Madangbawi offers an excellent view of downtown Seoul.

⛪ Water refill available at Cheonchuksa Temple spring. Madangbawi Rock is ideal for lunch.
3
Madangbawi Rock → Jaunbong Peak Junction
0.6km · 30min · ★★★★☆

Steep iron stairs and railings. Jaunbong Peak and Manjangbong Peak are in sight. At the junction, choose the direction to Sinseondae.

Weekend iron staircase congestion. Pass after yielding to descending hikers.
4
Junction → Sinseondae (714m)
0.3km · 15min · ★★★★☆

Final rock ridge. 360-degree view from the summit marker. Jaunbong, Manjangbong, Seoninbong Three Peaks Panorama.

Photo of Jaunbong Peak from Sinseondae = Dobongsan National Park's best photo spot
5
Sinseondae → Dobongsan Station (round trip)
3.5km · 80min · ★★★☆☆

Descend the way you came up. Return to the visitor center along Dobonggyegok Valley.

🍺 Dobongsan Station Specialty Street: Highly recommend Makgeolli, Dotori-muk, and Pajeon!
Essential Gear
  • Hiking boots for rocky terrain
  • Trekking poles (for iron stair sections)
  • Water 1.5L or more
  • Gloves (for gripping rocks)
  • Knee Pads
🍱
Meals/Drinks
  • Snack Break at Madangbawi Rock
  • Refill at Cheonchuksa Temple Mineral Spring
  • Dobongsan Station restaurant area after descent
  • Famous for soft tofu stew and samgyeopsal
Transportation Guide
  • Dobongsan Station (Subway Line 1 & 7), Exit 1
  • Station ~ Visitor Center 15 min walk
  • Car: Dobong-dong Parking Lot
  • Weekend parking is very congested
📅
Optimal Season
  • Optimal in Spring/Autumn (April-May, October)
  • Summer: Early start essential
  • Winter: Crampons essential
  • Early start recommended during autumn foliage season
Safety Warning
  • Absolutely no climbing on Jaunbong Peak
  • Yield alternately on the iron staircase for ascending and descending hikers
  • Caution: Strong winds at Sinseondae Peak
  • Night hiking prohibited

Total one-way approx. 7.2km · 4~5h · Mangwolsa Station → Dobongsan Station traverse

🚨 Advanced Course Warning:Y Valley strictly prohibited for beginners, children, and those with acrophobia. Rock climbing experience is essential. Operation suspended in case of rain or ice.
P
Depart from Mangwolsa Station
Altitude 85m · Subway Line 1, Mangwolsa Station

Mangwolsa Station (Subway Line 1) → 15-minute walk towards Simwonsa Temple. Final resupply at convenience store. Thorough equipment check.

🎯 Departure Check: Hiking boots, 2L water, gloves, knee pads, headlamp (for delays)
1
Mangwolsa Station → Enter Darakneungseon Ridge
1.5km · 45min · ★★★☆☆

Past Simwonsa Temple entrance, steep slope of Darakneungseon Ridge. Once on the ridge, continuous granite rocks. Significant incline from the start.

💪 The beginning is the most challenging. It's essential to maintain a slow pace.
2
Darakneungseon Rock Ridge Section
1.5km · 60min · ★★★★★

Dobongsan's best rock ridge. Continuous granite hand-and-foot scrambling. Continuous panoramic views of Seoul.

Beware of backpack getting caught on rocks. Maintain sufficient distance from the person in front. Detour is essential on slippery days.
3
Podae Observatory (600m)
0.5km · 20min · ★★★☆☆

Podae Ridge Observatory. Uijeongbu and Sapaesan Mountain at a glance. Y-Valley or detour option point.

🔋 Enter Y-Valley after sufficient rest and replenishing snacks. A detour option is also available.
4
Pass through Y Valley 🔥
0.5km · 30~40min · ★★★★★

Dobongsan National Park Highlights. Includes a section where you must remove your backpack to pass through a narrow Y-shaped rock crevice. Use ropes and iron bars. Pass through one person at a time.

🚨 Absolutely no beginners. Detach your backpack first, then pass. Detour essential in wet weather. Detour route: Left towards Sinseondae.
5
Y Valley → Sinseondae Peak (714m)
0.4km · 15min · ★★★☆☆

After passing Y Valley, follow the ridge for 200m to Sinseondae Peak. Peak sense of accomplishment. 360-degree panorama.

🏆 Sinsandae = Completion of Dobongsan National Park Advanced Badge! Summit certification photo required
6
Sinseondae → Descend to Dobongsan Station
3.5km · 75min · ★★★☆☆

Descend via Madangbawi Rock, Cheonchuksa Temple, and Dobongseowon Site. Walk to Dobongsan Station. Traverse complete!

🍺 Dobongsan Station Specialty Street: Highly recommend after-traverse celebration!
🚨 Advanced Course Essential Safety:Y Valley strictly prohibited for beginners and children. Experienced rock climbers must accompany. Discontinue in case of rain or ice. Avoid solo hiking.
Essential Gear
  • Hiking boots for rocky terrain (high friction)
  • Gloves required
  • Water 2L or more
  • Knee and Ankle Pads
  • Headlamp (for delays)
  • Long pants (knee protection)
⛑
How to pass Y Valley
  • Remove backpack first, then pass through
  • Maintain sufficient distance from person in front
  • Check footing before moving
  • Observe order one person at a time
  • Detour: Left option available
Traverse Transportation
  • Start: Mangwolsa Station (Line 1)
  • Arrival: Dobongsan Station (Line 1 & 7)
  • Transfer to Line 1 between the two stations
  • Traverse type — Public transportation recommended
📅
Optimal Season
  • Spring/Autumn (April-May, October)
  • Prohibited during summer monsoon season
  • Winter: Crampons and gaiters essential
  • Rock ridge is very slippery the day after rain
Safety Warning
  • Y Valley strictly prohibited for beginners
  • Caution: Backpacks may get caught on rocky ridges
  • Winter icing is very dangerous
  • Dobongsan Office 02-954-2566
