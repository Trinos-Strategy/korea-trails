📊 Elevation Profile — Yeongsil↑ Eorimok↓ Loop Traverse

Overall Course Flow
🅿 Yeongsil Trail Entrance→ Byeongpungbawi Rock→ Witseoreum 1,700m→ Nambyeok Junction→ Manse Dongsan→ Sajebidongsan→ 🅿 Eorimok Trail Entrance

Course Features

  • ✅ Yeongsil Trail entrance at 1,280m above sea level — high starting elevation allows for high-altitude scenery viewing over a short distance
  • ✅ Byeongpungbawi Rock strange cliffs + Obaeknahan, Hallasan National Park's best scenic section
  • ✅ Witseoreum Shelter sells cup noodles and drinks (cash required)
  • ✅ No reservation required (unlike Seongpanak and Gwaneumsa Temple, entry is possible without prior reservation)
  • Yeongsil Ticket Office → Yeongsil Trailhead (40 min walk or by car)
  • Baengnokdam summit is not accessible via this course — use intermediate or advanced courses for the summit
Detailed Route by Section
Departure
Depart from Yeongsil Trailhead
Elevation 1,280m · Hawon-dong, Seogwipo-si

Enter by car from Yeongsil Ticket Office or walk 40 minutes to reach the trailhead. Check safety regulations at the visitor center before starting. Use the restroom before ascending.

After parking at Yeongsil Ticket Office, it takes 40 minutes to walk to the trailhead. Shuttle bus available if operating. The earlier you depart, the more parking is available.
1
Yeongsil Trailhead → Byeongpungbawi
Approx. 1.5km · 50 min · Elevation ↑300m (Steep climb)
Difficulty

This course's only steep section is 'Kkaltakgogae'. After climbing a steep staircase for 40-50 minutes, you will reach Byeongpungbawi. The Obangnahan (Obaekjanggun) and strange rock formations unfold, offering the best scenery of Hallasan National Park.

This is the most challenging section of the course. Take plenty of breaks and ascend slowly. The subsequent Seonjakjiwat (plain) section is very gentle.
2
Byeongpungbawi Rock → Seonjakjiwat → Witseoreum Shelter
Approx. 1.3km · 50 min · Gentle plain
Difficulty

After passing Byeongpungbawi Rock, Seonjakjiwat (alpine plain) unfolds. This gentle, almost flat trail allows you to admire Hallasan National Park's unique alpine plants (Korean fir, azaleas). Cup noodles and drinks can be purchased at Witseoreum Shelter.

Cup noodles (1,500 won) and water can be purchased at Witseoreum Shelter. Cash is essential! Enjoy a special experience dining at outdoor tables with a view of Baengnokdam's south wall.
3
Witseoreum → Nambyeokbungi-jeom (Optional Viewpoint)
Round-trip approx. 1km · 30min · View of Baengnokdam South Wall
Difficulty

A round-trip hike from Witseoreum to Nambyeokbungi-jeom (0.5km) allows you to admire the magnificent volcanic cliffs of Baengnokdam's south wall head-on. The summit of Baengnokdam is not accessible from this point. After taking photos at Nambyeokbungi-jeom, begin descending towards Eorimok.

Nambyeok Junction is the closest point to view the southern wall of Baengnokdam. On a clear day, the grandeur of the volcanic cliff is overwhelming!
4
Nambyeok Junction → Manse Dongsan → Sajaebi Dongsan → Eorimok
Approx. 4.7km · 1h 40min · Descent
Difficulty

Descend towards Eorimok. Pass Manse Dongsan and Sajeebi Dongsan, then go through a dense fir forest to reach the Eorimok Trailhead. A gentle forest path continues after Sajeebi Dongsan.

Accessible by Jeju city bus from Eorimok Bus Stop. 15-minute walk from the trailhead to the bus stop. If using a private car, separate travel to Yeongsil Parking Lot is required.
Tips & Precautions

Essential Gear

  • Hiking boots (non-slip)
  • Trekking Poles
  • Windproof, waterproof jacket
  • Hat, Sunglasses
  • Headlamp
🥗

Food & Hydration

  • 1.5L or more of water
  • Energy bars & snacks
  • Prepare cash (shelter store)
  • Witseoreum Cup Noodle Sales
🛡

Safety Rules

  • Pass the trail entrance before entry control time
  • Winter season entry control 12:00, Summer season 15:00
  • No reservation required (Yeongsil·Eorimok)
  • Prepare for rapid changes in alpine weather

Transportation Information

  • Jeju City Center → Eorimok: Bus No. 240
  • Seogwipo → Yeongsil: Seogwipo City Bus
  • Yeongsil·Eorimok Parking Lot (Paid)
  • When doing a loop traverse, transportation planning for each direction is essential.
🌸

Seasonal Information

  • Spring (April-May): Azalea bloom peak
  • Summer: Mysterious sea of clouds at Seonjakjiwat amidst the clouds
  • Autumn: Eulalia and autumn foliage, best in October
  • Winter: Overwhelming snow scenery, crampons essential
📱

Useful Information

  • Hallasan National Park Reservation visithalla.jeju.go.kr
  • Entry Control Phone: 064-713-9950
  • Jeju Weather, Jeju Regional Meteorological Administration 064-752-0365
  • Use Rambler GPS App

📊 Elevation Profile — Seongpanak Baengnokdam Round Trip

Overall Course Flow
🅿 Seongpanak Trailhead Information Center→ Sokbat Shelter→ Saraoreum Entrance→ Jindallaebat Shelter→ Baengnokdam 1,947m→ (Return to starting point)→ 🅿 Seongpanak Return

Course Features

  • ✅ Course allowing ascent to Hallasan National Park's Baengnokdam Crater Lake summit (one of two)
  • ✅ Relatively gentle ridge — comparatively easier than Gwaneumsa Temple
  • ✅ Optional addition of Saraoreum Crater Lake exploration (round trip +1.3km)
  • One-way 9.6km · Round trip 19.2km — Sufficient physical stamina required
  • Seongpanak, Gwaneumsa Course advance reservation required (visithalla.jeju.go.kr)
  • Summer season entry control 12:30, Winter season 11:30 — Early departure is essential
Detailed Route by Section
Departure
Depart from Seongpanak Trailhead Information Center
Elevation 750m · 516-ro, Jocheon-eup, Jeju-si

Depart from the 516 Road (Seongpanak) Trailhead towards Jeju City. After confirming your reservation, begin your ascent. After using the restroom and checking your backpack, enter the trail.

Early departure between 5-6 AM is recommended. Including waiting time for summit photos (1-2 hours), the entire trip can take 12 hours.
No entry without advance reservation! You must make a reservation at visithalla.jeju.go.kr.
1
Seongpanak → Sokbat Shelter
Approx. 3.8km · 1h 20min · Elevation ↑300m
Difficulty

A wide and gentle trail continues. You will walk through dense Jeju warm-temperate forests and fir tree forests. Restrooms and water refills are available at Sokbat Shelter. This is an initial warm-up section.

Snacks and stretching are recommended at Sokbat Shelter. The altitude gradually increases from here.
2
Sokbat Shelter → Saraoreum Entrance → Jindallaebat Shelter
Approx. 4.0km · 1h 30min · Elevation ↑450m
Difficulty

The slope continues gently. After passing the Saraoreum Crater Lake entrance (round trip 1.3km exploration possible), you will arrive at Jindallaebat Shelter. The Jindallaebat area offers Hallasan National Park's best scenery when azaleas bloom in spring (late April to early May).

Be sure to replenish water at Jindallaebat Shelter. There is no drinking water until the summit. Replenish energy here and prepare for the final ascent.
3
Jindallaebat Shelter → Baengnokdam Summit
Approx. 1.8km · 1 hr 30 min · Elevation ↑550m (Steep incline)
Difficulty

The section from Jindallaebat to the summit is the most challenging. It involves repeated rocky terrain, stone paths, and steep inclines. At the summit, enjoy views of Baengnokdam crater lake and a 360-degree panorama. Waiting time for a summit marker photo is 30 minutes to over 1 hour.

Rapid weather changes are frequent in the summit area. A windproof jacket is essential for strong winds, fog, and rain. Crampons are required in winter.
4
Baengnokdam → Seongpanak (Descent)
9.6km · 3h~3h 30min · Return to starting point
Difficulty

Descend back to the starting point in the Seongpanak direction. Adhere to the descent control times (14:30 in summer, 13:30 in winter). Wear knee protectors and use two trekking poles to distribute knee impact.

Check descent time at Jindallaebat Control Post. When descending to Seongpanak, it is not possible to hike towards Gwaneumsa (not one-way).
Tips & Precautions

Essential Gear

  • Hiking boots (waterproof, non-slip)
  • Knee braces (essential)
  • 2 trekking poles
  • Windproof, waterproof jacket
  • Headlamp
🥗

Food & Hydration

  • 2L or more of water (limited refills mid-route)
  • No drinking water after Jindallaebat
  • Plenty of packed lunch/energy bars
  • Balanced Carbohydrate and Protein Snacks
🛡

Safety Rules

  • Departure between 5-6 AM is essential
  • Advance Reservation (visithalla.jeju.go.kr)
  • Strict adherence to entry control times
  • Prepare for rapid weather changes at the summit

Transportation Information

  • Jeju City Center → Seongpanak: Bus No. 181
  • Seogwipo → Seongpanak: Seogwipo City Bus
  • Seongpanak Parking Lot is paid (congested)
  • Early arrival for parking recommended

Reservation Information

  • Seongpanak, Gwaneumsa advance reservation required
  • Reservations for next month open on the 1st of every month at 09:00
  • First-come, first-served, closes early (within minutes on popular dates)
  • Note reservation cancellation penalties
🌡

Summit Environment

  • Summit temperature around 10°C in summer
  • Frequent strong winds and fog
  • Waiting time for summit marker photo 1-2 hours during peak season
  • Winter crampons and gaiters essential

📊 Elevation Profile — Gwaneumsa↑ Seongpanak↓ Traverse

Overall Course Flow
🅿 Gwaneumsa Trailhead Information Center→ Tamnagye Valley→ Gaemideung→ Samgakbong Shelter→ Baengnokdam 1,947m→ Jindallaebat Shelter→ Sokbat Shelter→ 🅿 Seongpanak Trailhead Information Center

Course Features

  • 🏆 Gwaneumsa (North) → Baengnokdam → Seongpanak (East) Hallasan National Park Full Traverse
  • ✅ Gwaneumsa Temple Course: Includes Tamna Valley, fir tree primeval forest, and Gaemideung scenic sections
  • ✅ Seongpanak descent: Safe descent along a gentle ridge
  • One-way 18.3km — A day traverse more challenging than a round trip
  • Advance reservation required for both Gwaneumsa Temple and Seongpanak (separate reservations for each on the same day)
  • Gwaneumsa Temple Course steep section 3.5km — for advanced hikers only
Detailed Route by Section
Departure
Depart from Gwaneumsa Trailhead
Elevation 620m · Ara-dong, Jeju-si

Travel from Jeju City Center towards Gwaneumsa. After confirming your reservation at Gwaneumsa Trailhead, begin your ascent. Depart after using the restroom.

Starting the ascent at 5 AM allows for a leisurely traverse. It is recommended to depart from Gwaneumsa by 7 AM at the latest.
Advance reservation required for both Gwaneumsa Temple and Seongpanak. Entry without reservation is not permitted!
1
Gwaneumsa Temple → Tamna Valley
Approx. 3.2km · 1h 10min · Elevation ↑300m
Difficulty

Walk along the trail through the dense Jeju warm-temperate forest to Tamna Valley. The atmosphere is a relatively gentle walking path. Crossing the Tamna Valley wooden bridge marks the beginning of the serious ascent.

Take a short break at Tamnagye Valley Wooden Bridge to replenish water and snacks. Afterwards, a steep 2.8km climb to Gaemideung Ridge begins.
2
Tamnagye Valley → Gaemideung Ridge → Samgakbong Shelter
Approx. 2.8km · 2 hr · Elevation ↑700m (Very steep incline)
Difficulty

The most difficult section of the Gwaneumsa Temple Course. A steep ridge climb, known as Gaemideung, continues relentlessly. Stairs and rocky ridges repeat, gaining 700m in elevation. Be sure to take ample rest at Samgakbong Shelter (1,500m).

This is the core difficult section of the course. There is a risk of injury after reaching the summit without knee protectors or trekking poles. It is essential to check the passing time at Samgakbong Control Post.
3
Samgakbong Shelter → Yongjingak Suspension Bridge → Baengnokdam Peak
Approx. 2.7km · 1 hr 30 min · Elevation ↑447m
Difficulty

From Samgakbong Shelter, cross the Yongjingak Suspension Bridge and ascend to the peak via the rocky ridge section. The magnificent volcanic terrain of the North Face and Janggumok, unique to the Gwaneumsa Course, unfolds. Reach the northern peak of Baengnokdam.

It is essential to confirm the descent route towards Seongpanak from the summit. Be careful not to confuse the descent directions for Gwaneumsa and Seongpanak!
4
Baengnokdam → Jindallaebat Shelter → Sokbat Shelter → Seongpanak
9.6km · 3h~3h 30min · Descent
Difficulty

Descend from the summit towards Seongpanak. After replenishing water and snacks at Jindallaebat Shelter, pass through Sokbat Shelter and arrive at Seongpanak Trailhead. This descent route is gentler than Gwaneumsa.

From Seongpanak, you can travel to Jeju City by Bus No. 181. If using the Gwaneumsa parking lot, vehicle retrieval is necessary — use a bus or taxi.
Tips & Precautions

Essential Gear

  • Hiking boots (waterproof, ankle support)
  • Knee braces (both sides essential)
  • 2 trekking poles
  • Windproof, waterproof jacket
  • Headlamp + spare batteries
  • Sunglasses, Sunscreen
🥗

Food & Hydration

  • 2.5L or more of water
  • Check water fountains at Samgakbong Peak and Jindallaebat
  • 2 meals worth of packed lunch/energy bars
  • Prepare sufficient high-calorie trail food

Reservation Information

  • Reservations for both Gwaneumsa Temple and Seongpanak
  • Separate reservation applications for each on the same day
  • visithalla.jeju.go.kr
  • Opens on the 1st of every month at 09:00, first-come, first-served
🛡

Safety Rules

  • Recommended to depart from Gwaneumsa at 5 AM
  • Must pass Samgakbong Peak before control time
  • Summer entry control strictly at 12:30
  • Solo hiking strictly prohibited

Transportation Information

  • Gwaneumsa Temple→Seongpanak One-way Traverse
  • Return by Bus No. 181 from Seongpanak
  • Vehicle retrieval required if parked at Gwaneumsa Temple
  • Taxi alternative when using a rental car
💪

Physical Preparation

  • Minimum 3-4 months of hiking training
  • Intermediate Course: Seongpanak Round Trip Pre-requisite
  • More than 5 hikes over 10km
  • Combine with knee and ankle strengthening exercises
