Bukhansan National Park Hiking Playbook

A guide to Bukhansan National Park, one of South Korea's best mountains. Provides detailed routes, real-world maps, and tips for the Beginner Bibong Ridge Course, Intermediate Baegundae Peak Course, and Advanced Mangyeongdae Ridge Traverse Course.

🌟 Course features
🚇
Public Transportation Access
Start directly from Bulgwang Station Exit 9. Easily accessible from anywhere in Seoul without a car.
👀
Monument to King Jinheung's Tour
Explore the replica of King Jinheung's Monument (National Treasure) just before the summit of Bibong Peak. Enjoy history and hiking together.
🪨
Rock Ridge Hotspot
The short rocky ridge section between Jokduribong Peak and Bibong Peak allows even beginners to experience the thrill of rock climbing.
🏛
Descent to Seunggasa Temple
Descend via the valley path past Seunggasa Temple, founded during the Silla Dynasty. A gentle finish to Gugi Ranger Post.
Start
Start 🚇 Bulgwang Station Exit 9
🏁 Elevation 110m
▼
TransportationBulgwang Station, Subway Line 6
AmenitiesConvenience Store, Restrooms
Exit 9, walk straight for about 500m to Daeho Apt. entrance (access road). Follow the signpost towards Bibong.
Section 1
①Bulgwang Station → Jokduribong Peak (370m)
📏 1.8km⏱ 50 minutes🟢 Easy
▼
GradientGentle → Steep
SurfaceDirt path + rocky ridge
At the Dulle-gil fork, choose the left path towards Jokduribong Peak. Use handholds on the short rocky ridge section just before the summit. Excellent views.
Section 2
②Jokduribong Peak → Hyangnobong Peak (535m)
📏 1.0km⏱ 30 minutes🟡 Moderate
▼
FeaturesConnecting rock ridges
ViewPanoramic view of Northwestern Seoul
The core section of the Bibongneungseon Ridge. A narrow rock ridge continues, offering panoramic views of Eunpyeong-gu. Gloves are convenient here.
Section 3
③Hyangnobong Peak → Bibong Peak ★ (560m)
📏 0.5km⏱ 20 minutes🟡 Moderate
▼
Key attractionsReplica of King Jinheung's Monument
View360-degree view of Northern Seoul
Bibong Peak is the highest point of the course (560m). A replica of the Monument to King Jinheung is at the summit. Excellent views in all directions. A good resting point.
Narrow rock ridge section just before Bibong Peak. Beware of the cliff on the right. Be especially careful in wet weather.
Section 4
④Bibong Peak → Samobawi Rock (500m)
📏 0.8km⏱ 25 minutes🟢 Easy
▼
FeaturesGiant rock formations, scenic viewpoints
GradientGentle Descent
A giant hat-shaped rock. Stone carvings related to Bukhansanseong Fortress remain. A great spot to rest on the flat rock surface.
Section 5
⑤Samobawi Rock → Seunggasa Temple → Gugi Ranger Post
📏 2.5km⏱ 55 minutes🟢 Easy
▼
Seunggasa TempleAncient temple founded in Silla Dynasty
Descent routeGentle descent along the valley
🌊 Gently descend while listening to the sound of the Seunggasa Valley stream. From Gugitam Explorer Center, take bus (7022·7211) to Gupabal Station.
Arrival
Arrival🏁 Gugitam Explorer Center (150m)
Bus Stop
▼
🎉 Hike completed! Take bus 7022 or 7211 → Gupabal Station (Subway Line 3) or take a taxi. Restrooms and convenience stores available.
Essential gear
Hiking boots (rocky sections), 1L+ water, hiking poles (optional), gloves, hat, sunscreen
🍱
Food & Drink
Water refill available at Seunggasa Temple's spring. Recommended to prepare snacks at a convenience store near Bulgwang Station before departure. No snack bar at the summit.
Transportation
Departure:Bulgwang Station, Subway Line 6, Exit 9
Arrival:Gugi Trailhead → Bus No. 7022·7211 → Gupabal Station (Line 3)
Safety Precautions
Caution: slippery rock ridge section just before Bibong Peak. Do not approach the cliff on the right. Rocks are twice as slippery on rainy days.
🌸
Recommended Season
Spring (azaleas·royal azaleas) / Autumn (foliage) are best. Early morning departure recommended in summer. Crampons essential in winter.
📱
App & Information
Utilize Smart National Park (official app), Korea Forest Service hiking maps, and Korea Meteorological Administration weather app. Visitor Information Center ☎02-909-0497

🌟 Course features
🏰
Bukhansanseong Cultural Heritage
Experience Joseon Dynasty mountain fortress culture by walking along the main gates and walls of Bukhansanseong Fortress, including Daeseomun Gate and Jungseongmun Gate.
🎯
Ascent to Seoul's highest peak
Baegundae Peak (836.5m) is the highest point in Seoul. The summit view of downtown Seoul + Gyeonggi-do is overwhelming.
🪢
Rope section
The section with steel ropes and stairs just before Baegundae Peak from Wimun Gate. It's short but steep, and requires patience as people often queue to ascend.
🌊
Sanseong Valley Scenery
A well-maintained trail along the valley, cool even in summer. A particularly beautiful forest path in spring and autumn.
Start🅿 Bukhansanseong National Park Service Center (230m)
🏁 Spacious parking lot
▼
ParkingBukhansanseong Public Parking Lot (Paid)
BusBus No. 704 → Get off at Sanseongipgu
Recommended to arrive before 7 AM on weekends — parking lots fill up quickly. If using the bus, take bus 704 from Gupabal Station (Line 3).
①Visitor Center → Daeseomun Gate (250m)
📏 0.5km⏱ 15 minutes🟢 Easy
▼
SurfacePaved road → natural path
FeaturesPass through Bukhansanseong Daeseomun Gate
🏰 Daeseomun Gate is the west gate of Bukhansanseong Fortress (built in 1711). Explore the fortress walls built during King Sukjong's reign of Joseon. The initial section is a gentle paved road.
②Daeseomun Gate → Borisa Temple (480m)
📏 1.4km⏱ 50 minutes🟡 Moderate
▼
SlopeGentle along the valley → gradually steeper
RestroomRestroom in front of Borisa Temple
A forest path where you can hear the sound of the valley stream. Water refill available at the spring just before Borisa Temple. Also passes through Jungseongmun Gate.
③Borisa Temple → Wimun Gate (780m)
📏 1.4km⏱ 60 min🔴 Steepness
▼
SlopeSteep stone stairs/rocky path
RequiredSection with Maximum Physical Exertion
⚡ This is the most challenging section of the course. You'll rapidly ascend over 300m in elevation. Sufficient rest and hydration are essential.
The Borisa~Wimun Gate section has steep stone stairs and rocky paths. Active use of hiking poles is recommended. Wear knee pads when descending.
④Wimun Gate → Baegundae Peak ★ (836.5m)
📏 0.3km⏱ 20 minutes🔴 Rock face
▼
FeaturesSections with steel ropes and stairs
View360-degree panoramic view of Seoul and Gyeonggi
The section from Wimun Gate to the summit is equipped with steel ropes and stairs. On weekends, there may be queues. Proceed slowly and safely.
Beware of slippery rock faces at the summit. Exercise caution in strong winds. Guides are stationed at good photo spots near the summit marker.
⑤Baegundae → Harujae Pass → Baegundae Exploration Support Center
📏 3.8km⏱ 80 min🟡 Moderate
▼
Descent routeHarujae Pass~Ui-dong Valley Path
BusBaegundae Exploration Center→Ui-dong
Baegundae → Descend Harujae Pass, then follow the valley path to Baegundae Exploration Support Center. Uisinseol Line Bukhansan Ui Station is accessible.
Arrival🏁 Baegundae Peak Explorer Center (330m)
Bus·Light Rail
▼
🎉 Completion! Baegundae Peak Explorer Center → Ui-dong bus or 20-minute walk → Bukhansan Ui Station (Ui-Sinseol Light Rail).
Essential gear
Hiking boots (ankle support), hiking poles (essential), knee pads, 1.5L+ water, trail food, gloves, hat
⏰
Departure Time
Recommended to start between 7-8 AM on weekends. Parking lots are full by 9 AM. If starting after 2 PM, calculate descent time carefully.
Transportation
Departure:Bus No. 704 → Sanseongipgu (Gupabal Station, Subway Line 3)
Arrival:Bukhansan Ui Station (Ui-Sinseol Line)
Safety Precautions
Wimun Gate~Summit: Beware of slippery rock faces. Knee injuries are frequent during descent. Always use trekking poles. Descend before sunset.
🌦
Check weather
Summit temperature is 5-7 degrees lower than the plains. Strong winds. Cancellation recommended if rain is forecast. Risk of lightning in summer.
📱
App & Reservations
National Park reservation not required (general course). Smart National Park app. Visitor Center ☎02-909-0497

🌟 Course features
👑
Samgakbong Peak Main Ridge
A key ridge traverse of Samgaksan, summiting both major peaks: Baegundae (836.5m) + Mangyeongdae (799.5m).
🧗
Essence of Rock Ridge Traverse
The Baegundae~Mangyeongdae section features rugged rocky ridges. Experience breathtaking views and the thrill of hand-over-hand climbing.
🏰
Bukhansanseong South Gate
Pass through numerous gates of Bukhansanseong Fortress, including Yongammun Gate, Daenammun Gate, and Bogungmun Gate. You can grasp the entire structure of the fortress.
Best View
Panoramic view of Baegundae, Insubong, and Mangyeongdae peaks from the Nojeokbong~Daenammun section. Bukhansan National Park's best viewpoint.
Start🚇 Ui-dong / Bukhansan Ui Station (160m)
Ui-Sinseol Light Rail
▼
TransportationUi-Sinseol Light Rail Bukhansan Ui Station
On foot20 minutes from the station towards Doseonsa Temple
This is a traverse course, so the starting and ending points are different. Public transportation is highly recommended. Departure between 6-7 AM is essential.
①Ui-dong → Doseonsa Temple (300m)
📏 1.8km⏱ 40 minutes🟢 Gentle
▼
Paved road ~ natural path. Restroom and spring water refill at Doseonsa Temple. Section for preparing for the main hike.
②Doseonsa Temple → Harujae Pass (540m)
📏 1.2km⏱ 50 minutes🟡 Steep
▼
🪨 A steep section with continuous stone steps. Take a short break at Harujae before challenging the summit.
Knee pads recommended for stair sections. Minimize backpack weight.
③Harujae Pass → Baegunbongammun (Wimun) → Baegundae Peak ★ (836.5m)
📏 1.5km⏱ 60 min🔴 Rock face
▼
Insubong Peak ViewBest at Harujae Pass
Summit Steel RopesWimun Gate~Summit Steep Slope
Baegundae Peak offers a 360-degree panorama of Seoul + Gyeonggi-do. After ample photos + rest, head towards Mangyeongdae.
The initial descent from the summit towards Mangyeongdae Peak is a very steep rocky ridge. There are sections where you may need to descend backward. Stay focused!
④Baegundae → Wimun Gate → Mangyeongdae ★ (799.5m)
📏 0.8km⏱ 50 minutes🔴 Rugged rocky ridge
▼
FeaturesSamgakbong Peak, the second highest peak
DifficultyMost Difficult Section of the Course
🧗 The highlight of this course. A rugged rocky ridge connecting Baegundae to Mangyeongdae via Uimun. Many sections require holding onto rocks to move.
There are unauthorized sections around Mangyeongdae. Always follow signposts and official trails. No deviations allowed.
⑤Mangyeongdae → Nojeokbong Peak (715m) → Yongammun Gate
📏 2.0km⏱ 60 min🟡 Moderate
▼
Best viewpoint for the panoramic view of Baegundae and Insubong peaks from Nojeokbong. Continue along the ridge after passing Yongammun (fortress gate).
⑥Yongammun Gate → Daenammun Gate (650m) → Descend to Gugigye-gok Valley
📏 4.0km⏱ 90 min🟢 Descent
▼
Daenammun GateBukhansanseong South Gate, good view
DescentGugigyegeok Valley~Gugi Trailhead
Descend towards Gugidong after Daenammun Gate. Gentle path along the valley. Continue using trekking poles.
Arrival🏁 Gugitam Explorer Center (150m)
Bus Stop
▼
🎉 Bukhansan National Park traverse completed! Take bus 7022 or 7211 → Gupabal Station (Line 3). Recommended to have a celebratory meal at a nearby restaurant.
🧗
Essential gear
Hiking boots (ankle-reinforced), 2 hiking poles, knee pads, gloves (for rock climbing), 2L+ water, trail food, emergency rations, headlamp
⏰
Departure Time
Must depart between 6-7 AM. 12km/6-8 hour course. 5:30 AM recommended in summer. Must plan to descend before sunset.
Transportation (A→B Traverse)
Departure:Ui-Sinseol Light Rail Bukhansan Ui Station
Arrival:Gugi Trailhead → Bus No. 7022·7211 → Gupabal Station
🆘
Emergency Contact
Bukhansan National Park ☎02-909-0497
Bukhansan National Park Mountain Rescue Team ☎02-996-1103
119 Mountain Rescue
🌦
Weather/Season
Spring and autumn are best. Crampons + gaiters are essential in winter. Due to lightning risk in summer, it is recommended to reach the summit before 12 PM. Cancel if rain is forecast.
💪
Fitness Level Standard
Recommended for hikers with 4+ monthly experiences. Cumulative elevation gain 1,100m, 12km. Significant knee impact during descent, so pre-train lower body strength.
