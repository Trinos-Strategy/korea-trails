A guide to Naejangsan National Park, one of South Korea's best-known mountains. Provides detailed routes, real-world maps, and tips for the Beginner Nature Observation Trail, Intermediate Seoraebong Peak Representative Course, and Advanced Ridge Traverse Course.

Course Flow

🚩 Iljumun Gate→🍁 Autumn Foliage Tunnel→⛩ Naejangsa Temple→Wonjeokam Hermitage→Bridge of Love→Byeongnyeonam Hermitage→🚩 Iljumun Gate

Elevation Profile

This is the least strenuous course in Naejangsan National Park, known as one of the first nature observation trails in a national park. It's perfect for families, the elderly, and those seeking a light stroll, as it allows you to explore the Autumn Foliage Tunnel, Naejangsa Temple, and Byeongnyeonam Hermitage together.

Sectional Route Guide

Exit
Depart from Iljumun Gate
0km · Group Facility Area
⌄

The hike officially begins at Iljumun Gate, past the parking lot, ticket office, and restrooms. From the start, maple trees and a wide trail continue, making it suitable as a warm-up section.

During peak autumn season, arriving before 9 AM is advantageous to avoid crowds.
1
Iljumun Gate → Autumn Foliage Tunnel
0.8km · 15 min
⌄

This is the Maple Tunnel section, a symbol of Naejangsan National Park. The slope is almost flat and wide, making it suitable for strollers and light walking.

It is best to take photos in the morning before the backlight.
2
Maple Tunnel → Naejangsa Temple
0.7km · 15 min
⌄

Enter the temple area along the valley and forest path. The area around Naejangsa Temple is a particularly popular spot for visitors in autumn.

Quiet viewing etiquette must be observed within the precincts of Naejangsa Temple.
3
Naejangsa Temple → Wonjeogam Hermitage → Sarangui Dari Bridge
1.3km · 25 min
⌄

This is the core section of the Nature Observation Trail. Gentle forest paths, streams, and bridges continue, allowing even beginners to walk safely.

The area around Wonjeokam Hermitage has a lot of shade, making it relatively cool even in summer.
Down
Return to Iljumun Gate via Byeongnyeonam Hermitage
1.1km · 25 min
⌄

After passing Byeongnyeonam Hermitage, you return to the starting point. The final section is also gentle, reducing strain on the knees.

After descending, the route to Naejangsan Cultural Plaza's cafes and restaurants is convenient.

Preparation & Gear

  • Light sneakers or trekking shoes
  • Water 500mL~1L
  • Light outerwear in autumn

Recommended Points

  • Maple Tunnel
  • Pond in front of Naejangsa Temple
  • Byeongnyeonam Hermitage Forest Path

Transportation

  • After Jeongeup Station, city bus No. 171, etc., are available.
  • Check shuttle operation during peak season
  • Alight at Naejangsan Tourist Complex and proceed on foot

Course Flow

🚩 Iljumun Gate→Byeongnyeonam Hermitage→Seoraebong Peak 624m→Bulchulbong Peak 622m→Wonjeokam Hermitage→🚩 Iljumun Gate

Elevation Profile

The Seoraebong Peak course is a representative trail of Naejangsan National Park, widely known as a 5.9km, approximately 4-hour course connecting Iljumun Gate, Byeongnyeonam Hermitage, Seoraebong Peak, Bulchulbong Peak, and Wonjeokam Hermitage. It is suitable as a representative route for intermediate hikers due to its excellent views of strange rock formations and rocky ridges, while not being excessively long.

Sectional Route Guide

Exit
Depart from Iljumun Gate
0km · Naejangsa Temple Area
⌄

This section involves entering towards Naejangsa Temple and warming up to Byeongnyeonam Hermitage. The beginning is gentle, but conserving energy before the main ascent is important.

Please check with the visitor center first for same-day access restrictions.
1
Byeongnyeonam Hermitage → Seoraebong Peak
1.8km · 1 hr 20 min
⌄

The Seoraebong Peak section features a continuous series of stairs and rocky ridge climbs. As its name suggests, it is characterized by a rocky ridge resembling a farm harrow.

After rain, rocks and fallen leaves can be slippery, so caution is needed.
2
Seoraebong Peak → Bulchulbong Peak
1.1km · 40 min
⌄

This is the core section with the most spectacular ridge views. From the rock observatory, you can see the peaks of Naejangsan National Park and the autumn foliage ridges at a glance.

After taking photos at the summit of Seoraebong Peak, it is recommended to take a long break in the Bulchulbong Peak section.
3
Bulchulbong Peak → Wonjeokam Hermitage
1.5km · 55 min
⌄

The descent section is continuously sloped, but the abundant forest shade slightly reduces the perceived burden. Using trekking poles is advantageous due to the strain on the knees.

After descending, it's good to quickly replenish water and snacks at Wonjeokam Temple.
Down
Wonjeokam Hermitage → Return to Iljumun Gate
1.5km · 1 hr
⌄

The trail concludes with a gentle promenade. During peak season, there may be large crowds from the descent to Iljumun Gate.

Easy access to the restaurant area in the collective facility zone makes it convenient for meals after hiking.

Gear

  • Trekking shoes with good grip
  • 1 or more trekking poles recommended
  • Water 1~1.5L

Caution

  • Many rocky ridge and stair sections
  • Slippery leaves after rain
  • Severe autumn crowd congestion

🚗 Transportation & Parking

  • Access via Jeongeup IC or Naejangsan IC
  • Parking lots 1-3 are paid, crowded during peak season
  • Accessible by city bus from Jeongeup Station

Course Flow

Iljumun Gate→Seoraebong Peak→Bulchulbong Peak→Manghaebong Peak→Yeonjibong Peak→Kkachibong Peak→Sinseonbong Peak→Yeonjabong Peak→Janggunbong Peak→Dongguri

Elevation Profile

The Ridge Traverse Course is introduced as an 11.7 km, approximately 7-hour thru-hiking route starting from Iljumun Gate and connecting Seoraebong Peak, Bulchulbong Peak, Manghaebong Peak, Yeonjibong Peak, Kkachibong Peak, Sinseonbong Peak, Yeonjabong Peak, Janggunbong Peak, and Dongguri. As it connects most of Naejangsan National Park's key peaks, it is suitable for experienced hikers for a day hike.

Sectional Route Guide

Exit
Iljumun Gate → Seoraebong Peak
2.8km · 1 hr 40 min
⌄

This is the first gateway of the Ridge Traverse. It involves a significant elevation gain early on, so pace control is important.

Starting early in the morning and reaching the first peak between 8-9 AM allows for a more relaxed pace.
1
Seoraebong Peak → Bulchulbong Peak → Manghaebong Peak
2.2km · 1 hr 20 min
⌄

This is a continuous ridge traverse section with excellent views, but short ascents and descents are repeated. It is a part where physical fatigue can easily accumulate.

In summer, there is little shade, leading to significant sun exposure.
2
Manghaebong Peak → Yeonjibong Peak → Kkachibong Peak
2.6km · 1 hr 30 min
⌄

This section offers the strongest feeling of traversing the main ridge of Naejangsan National Park. The views open up widely before and after Kkachibong Peak, making it a popular spot for photography.

It is best to take short, frequent breaks for snacks and hydration.
3
Kkachibong Peak → Sinseonbong Peak → Yeonjabong Peak
2.3km · 1 hr 20 min
⌄

Sinseonbong Peak is the highest peak of Naejangsan National Park and a highlight of the traverse. The ridge towards the upper cable car station gets closer, but careful walking is still required.

After a sufficient break with views from Sinseonbong Peak, prepare for the descent in the latter half.
Down
Yeonjabong Peak → Janggunbong Peak → Dongguri Descent
1.8km · 1 hr 10 min
⌄

This is a descent section with remaining elevation changes until the end. Ankle injuries are common due to accumulated fatigue, so concentration is required.

You should plan to complete your descent before sunset.

Essential Gear

  • Hiking boots, trekking poles, gloves
  • Water 1.5~2L
  • Plenty of lunch and snacks
  • Headlamp (for emergencies)

⏰ Time Plan

  • Recommended departure between 6-7 AM
  • Recommended lunch timing before/after Sinseonbong Peak
  • Aim to descend Dongguri before sunset

🚕 Transportation Operation

  • Confirm return route from endpoint in advance
  • Check connecting transportation for Jeongeup Station and parking lot
  • Check peak season shuttle and parking dispersion information
