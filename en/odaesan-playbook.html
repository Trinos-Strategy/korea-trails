📊 Elevation Profile — Seonjaegil Trail (Woljeongsa Temple → Sangwonsa Temple)

Overall Course Flow
🅿 Woljeongsa Temple Parking Lot→ 🌲 Fir Forest Trail (1km)→ Woljeongsa Temple Exploration→ Odaecheon Valley Path→ Seonjaegil forest path→ Sangwonsa Temple 970m ★→ Bus or reverse direction descent

Course features

  • 🌲 Pass through Korea's best fir forest trail (1km, 1,700 trees) — a refreshing forest tunnel all four seasons
  • ✅ Woljeongsa Temple (founded by Silla monk Jajang Yulsa in 643 AD) → 10km Odaecheon Valley path to Sangwonsa Temple
  • ✅ Gentle forest path with almost no incline — Includes sections accessible with strollers
  • ✅ Suitable for families, children, and the elderly / Best for walks during autumn foliage and snow blossom seasons
  • One-way 10km — Descend in reverse (Sangwonsa Temple → Woljeongsa Temple) or use the afternoon shuttle bus
  • Partial trail time restrictions for Sangwonsa Temple ~ Woljeongsa Temple section (check seasonally)
Detailed Route by Section
Departure
Depart from Woljeongsa Temple Iljumun Gate and Parking Lot
Elevation 600m · Jinbu-myeon, Pyeongchang-gun, Gangwon-do

Start from the parking lot in front of Woljeongsa Temple's Iljumun Gate in Odaesan National Park. Get a map at the visitor center, use the restroom, and then enter the Fir Forest Path. After parking, pay the Woljeongsa Temple admission fee (adults 4,000 won).

The Woljeongsa Temple admission fee includes access to the Fir Forest Path and Odaecheon Valley. Be sure to check the information map next to the ticket office before departing.
1
Fir Forest Path → Woljeongsa Temple Exploration
Approx. 1.0km · 25min · Flat
Difficulty

The 1km Fir Forest Path, stretching from Iljumun Gate to Woljeongsa Temple, is Korea's best forest tunnel, where over 1,700 giant fir trees cover the sky. It is refreshing all year round, and the snowy landscape in winter is overwhelming. Explore the Octagonal Nine-Story Stone Pagoda (National Treasure) within Woljeongsa Temple grounds.

The light in the Fir Forest Path is most beautiful in the early morning or around sunset. It is also famous as a seasonal photo spot.
2
Woljeongsa Temple → Odaecheon Valley Seonjaegil Trail
Approx. 6.5km · 2h · Valley forest path
Difficulty

Walk along Seonjaegil Trail (Path of Enlightenment), which follows Odaecheon Valley from Woljeongsa Temple to Sangwonsa Temple. It is a gently sloping forest path with almost no steep inclines, making it accessible to everyone. In summer, the sound of the cool valley water is refreshing; in autumn, the autumn foliage is beautiful; and in winter, the snowy landscape is superb.

Seonjaegil is named after the Buddhist 'journey of Sudhana's quest for enlightenment.' Rest areas and benches are provided along the way, allowing for a leisurely walk.
3
Arrive at Sangwonsa Temple, explore, and return
Elevation 970m · Select viewing/return method
Difficulty

Sangwonsa Temple, founded in 720, is a famous temple known as a prayer site for Manjusri Bodhisattva. It houses the oldest existing bronze bell in Korea (National Treasure No. 36) and the Seated Wooden Statue of Manjusri as a Boy (National Treasure). After exploring, descend in the reverse direction (Sangwonsa Temple → Woljeongsa Temple) or use the Sangwonsa Temple shuttle bus.

When descending from Sangwonsa Temple to Woljeongsa Temple in the reverse direction, it is the opposite direction of the Seonjaegil Trail, so you don't have to walk the same path again. Alternatively, you can conveniently return by shuttle bus from the Sangwonsa Temple parking lot.
Tips & Precautions

Essential gear

  • Sneakers or trekking shoes
  • Windproof jacket (valley coolness)
  • Winter: Crampons, warm gloves
  • Camera (fir forest, autumn foliage)

Transportation Information

  • Seoul Dongseoul Bus Terminal → Jinbu Bus
  • Jinbu → Odaesan bus service
  • Sangwonsa Temple → Woljeongsa Temple Shuttle Bus
  • Woljeongsa Temple parking 4,000 won, includes admission fee
🌸

Seasonal Attractions

  • Spring: Fresh scent of new growth fir trees
  • Summer: Odaecheon Valley is cool
  • Autumn: Autumn foliage peaks in October (among the best in Korea)
  • Winter: Spectacular fir forest in a snowy field
🛡

Safety rules

  • No cooking or swimming in the valley
  • Check Odaesan National Park entry control times
  • No entry outside designated exploration areas
  • Maintain silence within the temple
🍱

Food & Amenities

  • Recommended to carry 1L or more of water
  • Use restaurants near Woljeongsa Temple
  • Sangwonsa Temple snack bar available
  • Hwangtaegukbap (dried pollock soup) is famous in Jinbu-myeon
📱

Useful Information

  • Odaesan National Park 033-332-6417
  • Woljeongsa Temple 033-339-6800
  • Blackyak 100 Famous Mountains Certification (Birobong Peak)
  • Korea National Park Service reservation.knps.or.kr

📊 Elevation Profile — Sangwonsa Temple → Birobong Peak Round Trip

Overall Course Flow
🅿 Sangwonsa Temple Parking Lot→ Sangwonsa Temple Exploration→ Jungdaesajaam Hermitage→ Jeokmyeolbogung 1,400m→ Birobong Peak 1,563m ★→ (Return to starting point)→ 🅿 Return to Sangwonsa Temple

Course features

  • ✅ Odaesan National Park's shortest summit course — Starting from Sangwonsa Temple (970m), 3.5km one way
  • ✅ Passes through Jungdaesajaam Hermitage and Jeokmyeolbogung Temple (enshrining Sakyamuni's sarira) — Explore Buddhist holy sites
  • ✅ Panoramic view of Sangwangbong Peak, Horyeongbong Peak, Duryeongbong Peak, and Dongdaesan Mountain's Odae Ridge from Birobong Peak summit
  • ✅ Nation's best winter snow blossom course — Jeokmyeolbogung Temple to Birobong Peak hoarfrost scenery
  • Sangwonsa Temple ~ Jeokmyeolbogung Steep Stair Section requires significant physical exertion
  • Winter Birobong Peak summit: Beware of strong winds and wind chill
Detailed Route by Section
Departure
Depart from Sangwonsa Temple Parking Lot
Elevation 970m · Jinbu-myeon, Pyeongchang-gun, Gangwon-do

Start from the parking lot in front of the Sangwonsa Temple Trailhead Support Center (approx. 970m above sea level). Check the course and weather at the Trailhead Support Center before entering the mountain. Sangwonsa Temple is approximately 500m from the Sangwon Trailhead Support Center.

Sangwonsa Temple parking lot is paid (small vehicles 4,000 won). It fills up early on weekends. It is recommended to depart from Jinbu before 7 AM. Pay the entrance fee at the ticket office before entering the mountain.
1
Sangwonsa Temple → Jungdaesajaam Hermitage
Approx. 0.8km · 30min · Elevation ↑160m
Difficulty

The section climbing from Sangwonsa Temple to Jungdaesajaam Hermitage is a sloped staircase path. Pass Jungdaesajaam Hermitage (a central temple in Odaesan National Park) and climb higher. You can rest briefly at Jungdaesajaam Hermitage and look down at the Odaecheon Valley.

Be sure to see the Sangwonsa Temple Bronze Bell (National Treasure No. 36) and the Seated Wooden Statue of Manjusri as a Boy (National Treasure No. 221). The bronze bell is the oldest existing bronze bell in Korea.
2
Jungdaesajaam Hermitage → Jeokmyeolbogung
Approx. 0.8km · 30min · Elevation ↑270m
Difficulty

The section from Jungdaesajaam Hermitage to Jeokmyeolbogung is the steepest part of Odaesan National Park. Stairs are well-installed, but the incline is considerable. Jeokmyeolbogung (approx. 1,400m above sea level) is a sacred site enshrining the Buddha's sarira, visited by many hikers as well as Buddhists.

From Jeokmyeolbogung to Birobong Peak is a gentle ridge. Replenish energy here before starting the final 300m ascent.
3
Jeokmyeolbogung → Birobong Peak Summit
Approx. 1.9km · 50min · Elevation ↑163m
Difficulty

From Jeokmyeolbogung, a relatively gentle uphill climb continues along the ridge to Birobong Peak. Passing through a yew tree colony, you'll find a spectacular snow flower view in winter and a cool alpine forest path in summer. A 360-degree panoramic view is available from the summit of Birobong Peak (1,563m).

From the summit of Birobong Peak, you can witness the spectacular scenery of Sangwangbong Peak, Horyeongbong Peak, Durobong Peak, and Dongdaesan Mountain encircling it in a circular formation. On clear days, Seoraksan National Park is also visible.
4
Birobong Peak → Return to starting point
Approx. 3.5km · 1h 20min · Descent
Difficulty

Descend back to the starting point via the same path: Jeokmyeolbogung → Jungdaesajaam → Sangwonsa Temple. During the descent, you can once again enjoy the view of Odaecheon Valley from Jungdaesajaam.

Caution: steep stairs and slippery conditions when descending the section from Jeokmyeolbogung to Jungdaesajaam. Trekking poles are essential. Crampons required in winter.
Tips & Precautions

Essential gear

  • Hiking boots (non-slip)
  • Trekking pole
  • Windproof jacket
  • Winter: Crampons + winter clothing essential
🥗

Food & Hydration

  • Carry 1.2L or more of water
  • Energy bar/Lunch box
  • Water fountain near Sangwonsa Temple
  • Winter insulated beverage thermos
❄

Winter Snow Blossom Hike

  • Birobong Peak snow blossoms at their peak from December to February
  • Crampons required for all sections
  • Summit feels like -20°C or below
  • Winter gloves, hat, and hot packs are essential
🛡

Safety rules

  • Caution: steep slope between Jeokmyeolbogung and Birobong Peak
  • Strict adherence to mountain entry control times
  • Caution when moving on the summit during strong winds
  • Solo hiking discouraged
🌸

Spring Azaleas

  • Birobong Peak Azaleas peak in late May
  • Azalea colony between Jeokmyeolbogung and Birobong Peak
  • Spectacular scenery continuing to Sangwangbong Peak Ridge
  • Highest crowds in spring — early start recommended

Transportation Information

  • Dongseoul → Jinbu Intercity Bus
  • Jinbu → Odaesan entrance bus
  • Sangwonsa Temple Parking Lot paid 4,000 won
  • Odaesan National Park 033-332-6417

📊 Elevation Profile — Odae 5 Peaks Loop (Starting from Sangwonsa Temple)

Overall Course Flow
🅿 Sangwonsa Temple→ Birobong Peak 1,563m→ Sangwangbong Peak 1,491m→ Duryeong Pass→ Durobong Peak 1,422m→ Sinseonmogi→ Chadolbaegi→ Dongdaesan 1,434m→ Dongpigol→ 🏁 Return to Sangwonsa Temple

Course features

  • 🏆 Odaesan National Park's 5 major peaks: Birobong Peak, Horyeongbong Peak, Sangwangbong Peak, Durobong Peak, Dongdaesan Mountain connected loop hike
  • ✅ 26km total, complete in one day or loop back to Sangwonsa Temple for vehicle pickup
  • ✅ Duryeong Pass and Sinseonmogi Ridge — Passes through the core section of Baekdudaegan's Odaesan National Park
  • ✅ Dongdaesan Mountain descent via Dongpigol Valley → Seonjaegil Trail allows return to Sangwonsa Temple
  • 26km long-distance — Must depart Sangwonsa Temple between 5-6 AM
  • The section from Durobong Peak to Dongdaesan is sparsely populated and the trail is rough — team hiking with 3 or more people is recommended
Detailed Route by Section
Departure
Depart from Sangwonsa Temple at dawn
Elevation 970m · Recommended departure 5-6 AM

The Odae 5 Peaks Loop is a 26km long-distance hike. If returning to Seonjaegil from Dongpigol, park your car at Sangwonsa Temple or Woljeongsa Temple. Early morning departure between 5-6 AM is essential.

Save the course in a GPS app (Rambler, Trangle) in advance. Signposts are rare on the Durobong Peak-Dongdaesan Mountain section, so there's a risk of getting lost without GPS.
Solo hiking is strictly prohibited. There are sections on the 26km ridge where helicopter rescue is the only means of escape in case of an emergency.
1
Sangwonsa Temple → Birobong Peak (Intermediate Section)
Approx. 3.5km · 1h 40min · Elevation ↑593m
Difficulty

Same as the intermediate course. Sangwonsa Temple → Jungdaesajaam Hermitage → Jeokmyeolbogung → Birobong Peak. After a sufficient meal and energy replenishment at Birobong Peak, proceed east towards Sangwangbong Peak.

Headlamp is essential for early morning departures. The sunrise at Birobong Peak is the best experience on this course. If you time your arrival at the summit between 6-7 AM, it's a magnificent sight.
2
Birobong Peak → Horyeongbong Peak → Sangwangbong Peak
Approx. 4.5km · 1h 40min · Ridge traverse
Difficulty

Continue along the ridge from Birobong Peak, passing Horyeongbong Peak (1,561m) to Sangwangbong Peak (1,491m). Horyeongbong Peak is one of the highest peaks in Odaesan National Park, along with Birobong Peak (1,561m), offering outstanding views. Begin descent towards Duryeong Pass from Sangwangbong Peak.

Horyeongbong Peak is a short round trip from the ridge with no separate trail. It is one of Odaesan National Park's best scenic points. Be sure to visit.
3
Sangwangbong Peak → Duryeong Pass → Durobong Peak
Approx. 5.5km · 2h 30min · Baekdudaegan section
Difficulty

From Sangwangbong Peak, proceed along the Baekdudaegan Ridge via Duryeong Pass to Durobong Peak (1,422m). This is a sparsely populated section, and wild animals (wild boars, Asiatic black bears) may appear. Durobong Peak is a certified Baekdudaegan peak.

An escape route (towards Anseong or Gucheondong) exists at Duryeong Pass. Consider escaping if you experience physical difficulties. The section after Durobong Peak is a point of no return.
4
Durobong Peak → Sinseonmogi → Chadolbaegi → Dongdaesan
Approx. 7.0km · 2h 30min · Highest difficulty section
Difficulty

This is the most challenging section, from Durobong Peak, passing Sinseonmogi (ridge saddle) and Chadolbaegi, to Dongdaesan (1,434m). It's a deep forest ridge with wide gaps between signposts. From the summit of Dongdaesan, you can view the entire ridge of Odaesan National Park.

Chadolbaegi is named after its white 'chadol' (quartzite) rock formations. It's a viewpoint for appreciating unique rock formations.
5
Dongdaesan → Dongpigol → Seonjaegil → Sangwonsa Temple Return
Approx. 5.5km · 1h 40min · Descent
Difficulty

Descend from Dongdaesan to Dongpigol (upper valley of Ocheon Stream). From Dongpigol, follow Seonjaegil towards Sangwonsa Temple to return to the starting point. Relieve hiking fatigue while listening to the clear water of Dongpigol Valley.

The Dongpigol descent trail is steep. Knee pads are recommended. After connecting to Seonjaegil, it's a 2.7km flat walk along Ocheon Stream to Sangwonsa Temple.
Tips & Precautions

Essential gear

  • Hiking boots (waterproof, ankle support)
  • 2 trekking poles
  • Knee pads (both sides)
  • Headlamp + spare batteries
  • Winter: Crampons + winter clothing + hot packs
🥗

Food & Hydration

  • Carry 2.5L or more of water
  • Energy bar/Lunch box for 2 meals
  • Caution with drinking water in Dongpigol Valley (confirm potability)
  • After arriving at Sangwonsa Temple, Jinbu-myeon restaurant
📡

Navigation

  • GPS app required (Rambler, San-gil-saem)
  • Sparse signposts from Durobong Peak to Dongdaesan
  • Download GPX file in advance
  • Battery pack essential (for long-term use)
🛡

Safety rules

  • Early morning departure at 5-6 AM
  • Team hiking with 3 or more people required
  • Familiarize yourself with the Duryeong Pass escape route
  • Asiatic black bear habitat section — maintain noise
💪

Physical preparation

  • Intermediate course experience 10+ times
  • Long-distance training over 20km
  • Knee and ankle strengthening exercises
  • Sufficient sleep and hydration the day before

Ecological Information

  • Asiatic black bear habitat (Odaesan restoration)
  • Lenok/Goral habitat protection area
  • Cooking and camping strictly prohibited
  • Odaesan National Park, Korea's first carbon-neutral national park
