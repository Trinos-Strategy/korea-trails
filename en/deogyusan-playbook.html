📊 Elevation Profile — Hyangjeokbong Peak Round Trip via Gondola

Overall Course Flow
🅿 Muju Resort→ 🚡 Gondola ride (20 min)→ Seolcheonbong Peak 1,520m→ Hyangjeokbong Peak 1,614m ★→ 🚡 Gondola descent→ 🅿 Return to Muju Resort

Course Features

  • 🚡 Easiest course: Take the gondola up to Seolcheonbong Peak (1,520m) and walk only 0.6km
  • ✅ Deogyusan National Park's most accessible course, suitable for children, the elderly, and people with disabilities
  • ✅ Panoramic views of the main ridge connecting Jungbong and Namdeogyusan from Hyangjeokbong Peak
  • ✅ Best course for winter snow flower viewing — Seolcheonbong~Hyangjeokbong rime ice scenery
  • Gondola fare: 17,000 won (round trip) for adults, check in advance · Weekends tend to have long waits
  • Gondola operations may be suspended during strong winter winds — Prior weather check is essential
Detailed Route by Section
Departure
Departure from Muju Resort Gondola Boarding Area
Elevation 680m · Seolcheon-myeon, Muju-gun, Jeollabuk-do

Depart from the gondola boarding area within Muju Deogyusan Resort Ski Resort. Purchase a ticket (round trip adult approx. 17,000 won) and wait in line to board. It takes approximately 20 minutes to reach Seolcheonbong Peak (1,520m).

During weekends, holidays, and the snow flower season (Jan-Feb), expect wait times of over 1 hour for boarding. Arriving before 9 AM is recommended. Check gondola operating hours (winter season standard: 08:30~16:30).
1
Seolcheonbong Peak → Hyangjeokbong Peak Summit
One-way 0.6km · 20 min · Elevation ↑94m
Difficulty

From Seolcheonbong Peak, ascend to Hyangjeokbong Peak along a well-maintained deck trail. The slope is gentle, and stairs are well-installed, making it accessible to everyone. In winter, hoarfrost (snow blossoms frozen on trees) on both sides of the trail creates a spectacular sight.

The temperature at Hyangjeokbong Peak summit is approximately 5~8°C lower than in Muju city. Be sure to bring warm clothing for the perceived temperature.
2
Hyangjeokbong Peak Summit Exploration and View
Summit 1,614m · 30 min view
Difficulty

Hyangjeokbong Peak (1,614m) is the highest peak in Deogyusan National Park and the fourth highest peak in South Korea. From the summit, a panoramic view unfolds in all directions, including the main ridge leading to Jungbong Peak (1,594m) and Namdeogyusan Mountain, as well as Jeoksangsan Mountain. Inside Hyangjeokbong Shelter, you can rest warmly and purchase food.

Ramen and drinks are sold at Hyangjeokbong Shelter. Winter summit wind chill can drop below -20°C, so gloves and a hat are essential.
3
Hyangjeokbong Peak → Seolcheonbong Peak → Gondola Descent
0.6km · 15min · Descent
Difficulty

Return to Seolcheonbong Peak via the same path and descend using the gondola. You can enjoy the scenery of the Seolcheonbong Peak to resort section even inside the gondola.

There may be long waits for the descending gondola during peak season. It is essential to arrive at Seolcheonbong Peak before the last ride (16:30 in winter).
Tips & Precautions

Essential Gear

  • Winter Clothing, Padded Jacket (Low Summit Temperature)
  • Gloves·Scarf·Hat
  • Winter: Anti-slip crampons
  • Sunscreen (UV reflection from snow)
🚡

Gondola use

  • Round trip adult fare approx. 17,000 won
  • Operating hours 08:30~16:30 (winter season)
  • Operations suspended during strong winds or heavy snowfall
  • Call to confirm operation status before departure.
🌨

Snow Flower Season

  • Rime ice at its peak in January-February
  • Fantastic snow blossoms on the Seolcheonbong Peak ~ Hyangjeokbong Peak section
  • Crampons required (icy conditions)
  • Weekend wait times can exceed 2 hours

Transportation Information

  • Seoul → Dongseoul Bus Terminal → Muju Bus
  • Muju → Muju Resort Shuttle Bus
  • Daejeon → Muju Express Bus
  • Resort Parking Fee 5,000 won
🍂

Seasonal Attractions

  • Spring: Azaleas peak in mid-May
  • Summer: Cool ridge for summer retreat
  • Autumn: Fall foliage mid to late October
  • Winter: Best for snow flowers and rime ice
📱

Useful Information

  • Deogyusan National Park 063-322-3174
  • Muju Resort Gondola 063-320-7000
  • Blackyak 100 Great Mountains certification possible
  • Recommended to check sunrise times on an app for Hyangjeokbong Peak.

📊 Elevation Profile — Guchangdong Chilbong Peak Loop

Overall Course Flow
🅿 Guchangdong Trailhead→ Inwoldam Pond→ Chilbong Peak→ Seolcheonbong Peak→ Hyangjeokbong Peak 1,614m ★→ Jungbong Peak 1,594m→ Dongyeopnyeong Pass→ Gucheondonggyegok Valley→ 🅿 Return to Guchangdong

Course Features

  • ✅ Passes through all 33 scenic spots of Deogyusan National Park's Gucheondong Valley (Bipadam, Guwoldam, Isokdae, etc.)
  • ✅ Dual landscape of Gucheondong Valley and Hyangjeokbong Ridge from Chilbong Ridge
  • ✅ Loop hike starting and ending at Gucheondong Exploration Support Center — No vehicle transfer needed
  • ✅ Excellent views towards Namdeogyu from the Jungbong (1,594m)~Dongyeopnyeong ridge
  • 18km long distance — Recommended to depart before 8 AM
  • Caution: steep sections on Chilbong Ridge / Crampons are essential for all sections in winter.
Detailed Route by Section
Departure
Gucheondong Visitor Support Center Departure
Elevation 600m · Anseong-myeon, Muju-gun, Jeollabuk-do

Depart from Muju Guchangdong Visitor Center. After checking the course and using the restroom at the information center, begin your ascent. Parking is available (paid).

Gucheondong is located in Gucheondonggyegok Valley, Seolcheon-myeon, Muju-gun, and can be reached by bus from Muju. The Samgong-ri area has a high concentration of guesthouses and pensions.
1
Gucheondong → Inwoldam → Chilbong Peak
Approx. 5.0km · 2h · 33 Scenic Valley Section
Difficulty

Follow Gucheondonggyegok Valley, enjoying the Gucheondong 33 Scenic Spots such as Inwoldam and Bipadam, as you ascend gently. The slope becomes significantly steeper once you climb onto the Chilbong Ridge at the Chilbong Peak junction.

Information signs for the Gucheondong 33 Scenic Spots are installed, making it enjoyable to identify each valley landscape as you walk. The autumn foliage, snow flowers, and fresh greenery offer diverse scenery depending on the season.
2
Chilbong Peak → Seolcheonbong Peak → Hyangjeokbong Peak Summit
Approx. 4.5km · 2h · Approach to main ridge
Difficulty

The section from Chilbong Ridge to Seolcheonbong Peak is an access route to the main ridge of Deogyusan National Park with a considerable incline. You will join tourists descending from the gondola at Seolcheonbong Peak, followed by a final 0.6km climb to Hyangjeokbong Peak.

Meals are available at Hyangjeokbong Shelter from Seolcheonbong Peak. It is recommended to replenish energy sufficiently before descending towards Jungbong Peak.
3
Hyangjeokbong Peak → Jungbong Peak → Dongyeopnyeong Pass
Approx. 4.5km · 1h 30min · Main ridge descent
Difficulty

From Hyangjeokbong Peak, follow the main ridge past Jungbong Peak (1,594m) to Dongyeopnyeong Pass. The ridge view towards Namdeogyusan Mountain from Jungbong Peak is breathtaking. Descend towards Guchangdong Valley from Dongyeopnyeong Pass.

Some say the view from Jungbong Peak is even better than from Hyangjeokbong Peak. On a clear day, Jirisan National Park and Gayasan National Park are visible.
4
Dongyeopnyeong Pass → Guchangdong Valley → Return to Guchangdong
Approx. 4.0km · 1h 30min · Valley descent
Difficulty

Descend from Dongyeopnyeong Pass towards Anseong District or Guchangdong. When descending towards Guchangdong Valley, you can visit Baengnyeonsa Temple. Baengnyeonsa Temple (founded by King Heungdeok of Silla) is the highest nunnery in Korea (970m).

Approximately 4km valley trail from Baengnyeonsa Temple to Guchangdong Visitor Center. If you have time, slowly enjoy the 33 scenic spots of the valley as you descend.
Tips & Precautions

Essential Gear

  • Hiking Boots (Waterproof)
  • 2 trekking poles
  • Windproof, Waterproof Jacket
  • Winter: Crampons + gloves
🥗

Food & Hydration

  • Water 1.5L or more
  • Ramen available for purchase at Hyangjeokbong Shelter
  • Lunchbox, Energy Bar
  • Baengnyeonsa Temple Mineral Water Available
🛡

Safety Rules

  • Depart between 7-8 AM (for 18km course)
  • Slippery descent from Dongyeopnyeong Pass to Baengnyeonsa Temple
  • Plan to complete descent before sunset
  • Check mountain entry control times
🌊

Gucheondong 33 Scenic Spots

  • Naje Tongmun Gate·Inwoldam·Bipadam
  • Guwoldam·Isokdae·Ansimdae
  • Guchangpokpo Waterfall in front of Baengnyeonsa Temple
  • Do not drink stream water
🌸

Seasonal Characteristics

  • Spring: Gucheondong's fresh greenery + azaleas
  • Summer: Best for refreshing valleys
  • Autumn: Fall foliage peaks in October
  • Winter: Overwhelming snow flower ridge

Transportation Information

  • Bus Service from Muju to Guchangdong
  • Parking available at Samgong-ri (Gucheondong Entrance)
  • Seoul→Dongseoul Bus Terminal→Muju
  • Muju-gun Tourism 063-320-5643

📊 Elevation Profile — Yukgujongju (Guchangdong → Yuksipnyeong Pass)

Overall Course Flow
🅿 Guchangdong→ Baengnyeonsa Temple→ Hyangjeokbong Peak 1,614m→ Jungbong Peak→ Baegambong Peak→ Dongyeopnyeong Pass→ Muryongsan Mountain 1,491m→ Satgatjae Shelter→ Satgatbong Peak→ Namdeogyusan Mountain 1,507m→ 🏁 Yuksipnyeong Pass

Course Features

  • 🏆 Deogyusan National Park Full Ridge Traverse — Guchangdong (Gu-side) → Yuksipnyeong Pass (Yuk-side), aptly named 'Yukgujongju'
  • ✅ Passes through 6 peaks: Hyangjeokbong (1,614m), Jungbong (1,594m), Baegambong (1,503m), Muryongsan (1,491m), Satgatbong (1,418m), and Namdeogyusan (1,507m)
  • ✅ Can be split into a 2-day, 1-night course with an overnight stay at Satgatjae Shelter (1,280m)
  • ✅ Passes through the core section of the Baekdudaegan mountain range — Dongyeopnyeong~Namdeogyusan section
  • Approx. 27km one-way long distance — advance preparation for transportation at the Yuksipnyeong endpoint is essential.
  • For advanced hikers with sufficient stamina — recommended to attempt after gaining ample intermediate experience.
Detailed Route by Section
Departure
Gucheondong Visitor Center Departure (Dawn)
Elevation 600m · Muju-gun, Jeollabuk-do

For a same-day completion of the Yukgujongju traverse, a pre-dawn departure between 4-5 AM is essential. Be sure to pre-book transportation or arrange a car shuttle from Yuksimnyeong Pass (the endpoint) towards Jangsu/Jeonju.

Recommended 2-day, 1-night trip: Day 1 Gucheondong → Satgatjae Shelter, Day 2 Satgatjae → Namdeogyu → Yuksipnyeong. Satgatjae Shelter reservations: 063-322-4536.
Solo hiking is strictly prohibited. Rescue is difficult in case of an accident on the 27km ridge section. Team hiking of 3 or more people is recommended.
1
Gucheondong → Baengnyeonsa Temple → Hyangjeokbong Peak
Approx. 9km · 3h 30min · Intermediate section
Difficulty

Follow the Gucheondong 33 Scenic Valley past Baengnyeonsa Temple and Hyangjeokbong Shelter to the summit of Hyangjeokbong Peak. From Hyangjeokbong Peak, begin the Baekdudaegan main ridge traverse towards Jungbong Peak.

Have a sufficient meal and replenish energy at Hyangjeokbong Peak summit. Afterward, a long ridge hike of approximately 13km continues to Satgatjae Pass.
2
Hyangjeokbong Peak → Jungbong Peak → Baegambong Peak → Dongyeopnyeong Pass
Approx. 6km · 2h 30min · Baekdudaegan Ridge
Difficulty

From Hyangjeokbong Peak, follow the ridge past Jungbong Peak (1,594m) and Baegambong Peak (1,503m) to Dongyeopnyeong Pass. Enjoy expansive views of Jirisan National Park, Gayasan National Park, and Jeoksangsan Mountain from the open ridge. Dongyeopnyeong Pass is a key saddle connecting Anseong and Guchangdong.

There is an escape route descending towards Anseong from Dongyeopnyeong Pass. Consider escaping if you experience physical difficulties. Descend to Chilyeon Valley, approximately 4km to Anseong Visitor Center.
3
Dongyeopnyeong Pass → Muryongsan Mountain → Satgatjae Shelter
Approx. 6km · 2h 30min · Plateau ridge
Difficulty

From Dongyeopnyeong Pass, cross Muryongsan Mountain (1,491m) and follow the plateau ridge to Satgatjae Shelter (1,280m). The ridge around Muryongsan Mountain is exceptionally wide and open, allowing you to experience the true essence of the Deogyu ridge.

If staying overnight at Satgatjae Shelter, this is the end of Day 1. Shelter reservation is required. On Day 2, descend from Namdeogyu to Yuksipnyeong.
4
Satgatjae → Satgatbong Peak → Namdeogyusan → Seobong Peak → Yuksipnyeong
Approx. 8km · 3h 30min · Final section
Difficulty

Cross Satgatbong Peak (1,418m) to reach the summit of Namdeogyusan (1,507m). Namdeogyusan is the second highest peak of Deogyusan National Park, offering a distinct landscape from Hyangjeokbong Peak. Descend steeply past Seobong Peak (1,492m) to Yuksipnyeong (700m) to complete the traverse.

Buses are available from Yuksimnyeong Pass towards Jangsu, Jeonju, and Daejeon, but the intervals are long. Advance taxi reservation is strongly recommended. Yuksimnyeong Pass is right next to the border of Jangsu-gun and Hamyang-gun.
Tips & Precautions

Essential Gear

  • Hiking Boots (Waterproof, Ankle Support)
  • 2 trekking poles
  • Knee Protectors (Both Sides)
  • Headlamp + spare batteries
  • Winter: Crampons + gaiters
🥗

Food & Hydration

  • Water 2.5L or more
  • Ramen, drinks at Hyangjeokbong Shelter
  • Meals available at Satgatjae Shelter
  • Prepare lunch for 2 meals
🛖

Satgatjae Shelter

  • Phone: 063-322-4536
  • Reservation: National Park Reservation System
  • Limited capacity, advance reservation required
  • 1 night, 1 person: 13,000 won (adult)
🚗

Transportation Plan

  • Gucheondong Departure → Yuksipnyeong Arrival
  • Yuksimnyeong Pass → Jangsu·Jeonju Bus (infrequent)
  • Advance taxi reservation for Yuksimnyeong Pass is essential
  • Vehicle teams are recommended to park at Yuksipnyeong.
💪

Physical Preparation

  • Complete intermediate courses 10+ times beforehand
  • Long-distance training over 25km
  • Knee and Ankle Strengthening Exercises
  • Sufficient sleep and hydration the day before

Attractions Information

  • Gucheondong 33 Scenic Valley Landscape
  • Jungbong Peak and Baegambong Peak Ridge View
  • Muryongsan Mountain Wide Plateau Ridge
  • Namdeogyusan Mountain~Seobong Peak Scenic View
